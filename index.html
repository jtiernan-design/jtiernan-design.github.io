Official Working Document

The Draft Resolution

This is the document you will debate, amend, and vote on. Read every clause carefully — your amendments must reference specific clause numbers.

PREAMBLE — Why we are here

1.1
Deeply concerned with greenhouse gas build-up and its impact on Earth's climate
1.2
Aware that greenhouse gases are largely the result of human activities, including burning fossil fuels
1.3
Alarmed that all countries — especially developing nations and small island states — face increased climate risks
1.4
Reiterating that climate action must be integrated with social/economic development and poverty eradication
1.5
Keeping in mind the world's continued dependence on energy, food, and transport systems that drive emissions
1.6
Guided by a commitment to long-term practical decisions for the safety of future generations
1.7
Emphasises that young people's representation in climate negotiations is essential
1.8
Recognising that vulnerable peoples are disproportionately affected by climate change, including food rights

OPERATIVE — What we agree to do

2.1
Urges all nations to commit to Paris Agreement goals and increase emission reduction targets to stay below +2°C
2.2
Requests wealthy nations invest in renewable energy (wind, solar, hydro) and phase out coal, gas, oil and nuclear
2.3
Calls upon all nations to protect forests and invest heavily in reforestation to absorb CO₂
2.4
Invites States to implement sustainable agriculture and hold the sector accountable for its climate impacts
2.5
Encourages all States to promote plant-based diets to reduce emissions and improve food security
2.6
Suggests wealthy nations increase contributions to the Green Climate Fund for adaptation in developing countries
2.7
Further reminds States to provide green jobs when transitioning from fossil fuels and eliminate inefficient subsidies
2.8
Appeals to all States to increase Indigenous peoples' representation in climate negotiations
2.9
Recommends Member States recognise the right to a clean, healthy environment as a human right

Speaking in the Assembly

Speech & Phrase Guide

All delegates speak in German on conference day. Use these phrases throughout. You must address the Secretary-General as "Ehrenwerte/r Herr/Frau Generalsekretär/in" and fellow delegates as "geschätzte Kolleginnen und Kollegen".

📋 Position Statement Structure (2 minutes)

1. Open with a formal greeting
2. State your country's position in 1–2 sentences
3. Give 2–3 specific points about your country's situation/policies
4. State what you support or oppose in the Draft Resolution
5. Signal your amendment intentions
6. Close formally — thank the assembly

🟦 Opening & Formal Phrases

"Vielen Dank, ehrenwerte/r Herr/Frau Generalsekretär/in und geschätzte Kolleginnen und Kollegen der Vollversammlung."Thank you, Honourable Secretary-General and distinguished delegates.
"[Land] möchte seine Position zu diesem wichtigen Thema darlegen."[Country] would like to present its position on this important topic.
"Die Delegation von [Land] begrüßt diese Gelegenheit zur Diskussion."The delegation of [Country] welcomes this opportunity for discussion.
"[Land] möchte sich auf die Ausführungen der Delegation von [anderes Land] beziehen."[Country] would like to respond to the statement of the delegation of [other country].

🟩 Supporting / Agreeing

"[Land] unterstützt diesen Vorschlag nachdrücklich."[Country] strongly supports this proposal.
"[Land] stimmt dieser Ansicht vollständig zu."[Country] fully agrees with this view.
"[Land] begrüßt die Initiative von [anderes Land]."[Country] welcomes the initiative of [other country].
"[Land] ist bereit, diesen Änderungsvorschlag zu unterstützen."[Country] is prepared to support this amendment.
"In Übereinstimmung mit [anderes Land] ist [Land] der Meinung, dass..."In agreement with [other country], [Country] believes that...

🟥 Opposing / Disagreeing

"[Land] kann diesem Vorschlag in der vorliegenden Form nicht zustimmen."[Country] cannot agree to this proposal in its current form.
"[Land] ist besorgt über die möglichen Auswirkungen dieser Maßnahme."[Country] is concerned about the possible impacts of this measure.
"[Land] lehnt diese Klausel ab, da sie..."[Country] rejects this clause because it...
"[Land] möchte betonen, dass dieser Vorschlag die Entwicklungsinteressen nicht berücksichtigt."[Country] wishes to emphasise that this proposal does not consider development interests.

🟨 Proposing Amendments / Negotiating

"[Land] schlägt folgende Änderung zu Klausel [Nummer] vor..."[Country] proposes the following amendment to clause [number]...
"[Land] beantragt eine unmoderierte Sitzung von [5/10] Minuten."[Country] moves for an unmoderated caucus of [5/10] minutes.
"Würde [anderes Land] bereit sein, diesen Änderungsvorschlag zu unterstützen?"Would [other country] be willing to support this amendment?
"[Land] schlägt einen Kompromiss vor: ..."[Country] proposes a compromise: ...
"Gibt es weitere Länder, die diesen Vorschlag unterstützen möchten?"Are there other countries who wish to support this proposal?

How to Change the Resolution

The Amendment Guide

An amendment is a change to a clause in the Draft Resolution. This is where your diplomatic skill really matters. You're changing the official working document of the assembly.

1

Choose your target clause

Re-read the Draft Resolution and identify which clause most affects your country's interests. You can modify an existing clause, add a new clause, or propose removal of a clause. Pick battles that matter to your country.

2

Choose your strategy — strengthen or soften?

Use powerful operative words to strengthen a clause, or softer words to weaken it:

To strengthen:

Demands Condemns Strongly urges Declares Calls upon Decides

To soften:

Encourages Invites Suggests Considers Notes with interest Expresses the hope
3

Write your amendment clearly

Show exactly what is being crossed out and what is being added. Be precise — ambiguous amendments will be challenged in debate. Strike through text you remove and clearly mark text you insert.

4

Find a seconder

Every amendment needs at least one other country to support it before it can be debated. Use the caucus session and breaks to find your supporters. Tell them why this amendment benefits their country too.

5

Submit before lunch

All amendments must be submitted on the official UNAAV amendment slip before the end of the caucus session (before 12:30pm). Fill it in clearly and hand it to the Secretariat.

✦ Example Amendment Slip
Clause No: 2.6
Proposer (Country): Vanuatu
Seconded by: Barbados, Kenya
Original Clause:
Suggests all wealthy developed States tackle the causes of climate change and increase their financial support to the Green Climate Fund…
Proposed Amendment:
Suggests Demands all wealthy developed States tackle the causes of climate change and increase their financial support to the Green Climate Fund, providing a minimum of $100 billion annually by 2025, with a binding commitment to scale this to $300 billion annually by 2030…

🔴 Crossed-out text = deleted  |  🟢 Green/bold text = inserted

💡 Amendment Tips

• Prepare 1–2 amendments before the conference day
• Amendments requiring the most support from other countries should be moderate/compromise positions
• In the amendment session, you have limited time to argue your case — prepare a 1-minute defence speech
• If your amendment passes, it becomes part of the final resolution — real impact!

Rules of the Assembly

Conference Protocol

Follow these rules throughout the entire conference. The Secretary-General enforces them.

🪧

Raise your country placard — never your hand — to request to speak. Wait for the Secretary-General to call on you.

🧍

Stand when speaking. Always say your name and country before making any statement.

🎙️

Address the Secretary-General as "Ehrenwerter Herr Generalsekretär" or "Ehrenwerte Frau Generalsekretärin" and fellow delegates as "geschätzte Kolleginnen und Kollegen".

🌍

Never say "I" — always refer to yourself as your country. Not "I think..." but "The delegation of Denmark believes..." Never say "he/she/they" about another delegate — use their country name.

⏱️

Position Statements: 3 minutes max. Caucus speeches: 2 minutes. You will be cut off. Practise your speech aloud before the day.

🤝

Represent your country, not yourself. Put your personal views aside. You are an ambassador — argue your country's interests.

📱

Devices allowed for research — use them to look up facts during caucus sessions if challenged. But do not use AI to write your responses on the day.

📝

Fill in your Friend or Foe sheet during Position Statements — note which countries share your views and which oppose you. This guides your alliances.

"Vielen Dank, ehrenwerte Frau Generalsekretärin und geschätzte Kolleginnen und Kollegen der Vollversammlung. Die Delegation von Dänemark möchte ihre klare Position zum Klimawandel darlegen. Dänemark erzeugt über 80 Prozent seines Stroms aus Windenergie und hat sich rechtlich verpflichtet, bis 2050 klimaneutral zu sein. Wir unterstützen das 1,5-Grad-Ziel des Pariser Abkommens als absolute Untergrenze und rufen alle Industrieländer auf, ihre Emissionsreduktionsziele zu verschärfen. Dänemark beabsichtigt, Klausel 2.1 dahingehend zu ändern, dass das 1,5-Grad-Ziel ausdrücklich genannt wird. Wir danken der Vollversammlung für ihre Aufmerksamkeit."

ENGLISH (for reference)

"Thank you, Honourable Secretary-General and distinguished delegates. The delegation of Denmark wishes to present its clear position on climate change. Denmark generates over 80% of its electricity from wind power and has a legally binding commitment to carbon neutrality by 2050. We support the 1.5°C Paris Agreement goal as an absolute minimum and call on all industrialised nations to strengthen their emissions reduction targets. Denmark intends to amend Clause 2.1 to explicitly name the 1.5°C target. We thank the Assembly for its attention."
